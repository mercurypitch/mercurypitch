## 1. Brand direction — "Liquid Precision"
The concept: **Mercury = quicksilver** (liquid metal, fluid, reflective, *responds instantly*) —
which mirrors what the product does: turns your voice into something visible, in real time, with
sub-cent accuracy. Mercury is also the fastest planet and the messenger god who (in myth) invented
the lyre. Speed + communication + music + liquid metal, all in one word. "Pitch" = the sine wave /
frequency axis of music.

- **Personality:** sleek pro-tech with a quicksilver-premium edge.
- **Brand promise:** *Your voice, made visible — fluid, immediate, precise.*
- **Tagline (primary):** **"Find your pitch."** (alts: "Your voice, in perfect focus." / "See every note.")

Built **additively on the existing app design system** (obsidian `#0d1117` + blue/teal/violet already
in `src/styles/app.css`), adding a **Mercury/chrome** family. Zero migration cost.

## 4. Chosen mark — Sound Globe (V15) finalization
**Source of truth:** `logo/soundglobe/` (all generated by `scripts/soundglobe.py` + `finalize_sg.py`).

Design: a 3D "wireframe globe of sound" — **5 calm spectrum waveform latitude bands** (amplitude
bulging at the equator, opacity fading toward the poles), **two meridian rings** for the 3D globe
read, a spectrum rim, on an obsidian radial disc. A simplified **3-wave compact** variant swaps in at
≤32px so it never turns to mush.

### Iteration history (so we don't repeat mistakes)
1. First finalize removed the meridian and added a **white** equator highlight, and used 7 twisting waves.
2. User feedback: *"the orb is too busy, too many waves, reduce it."* → cut to 5 bands, 2-cycle (calmer).
3. User feedback: *"not alike globe with that middle ring… 3D aspect that was nice… middle line is
   stupid white color."* → **restored the meridian rings** (3D globe), **removed the white line**
   entirely (equator is now just the boldest spectrum wave).
4. Current state = the version that satisfied that feedback. **Do not re-add the white line; keep the
   meridians.**

### To retune (if asked)
Edit `scripts/soundglobe.py`: `BANDS` (count/amplitude/width/opacity), `PHASE` (twist),
`meridians()` (globe rings). Then re-run `soundglobe.py` → `finalize_sg.py` → `build_showcase.py`.

## 7. Trademark (full guide in `TRADEMARK.md`)
Route for an EU-based founder wanting worldwide coverage:
1. Register the **word mark "MercuryPitch"** (standard characters) — broadest protection.
2. **EUIPO** base filing (covers all 27 EU states) — ~€1,050 for 3 classes, ~4–6 months.
3. Expand via **Madrid Protocol (WIPO)** — one international app designating US, UK, etc.
   (⚠️ EUTM does **not** cover the UK post-Brexit — designate it separately).
4. **US (USPTO)** — strict; **a US-licensed attorney is mandatory** for foreign applicants; use-based.
5. **Classes:** 9 (software) + 42 (SaaS) + 41 (training/education).
6. **Clearance first** — "Mercury" is crowded (esp. class 41 / music, e.g. Mercury Records).
7. File foreign apps **within 6 months** of the EU filing to claim priority.
*Not legal advice — brief an attorney for the global rollout.*
