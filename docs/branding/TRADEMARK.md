# MercuryPitch — Trademark Registration Guide

> Practical playbook for protecting the **wordmark “MercuryPitch”** globally, written for an
> **EU-based founder** who wants worldwide coverage.
>
> ⚠️ **Not legal advice.** This is general information to help you brief a trademark attorney and make
> informed decisions. “Mercury” is a common word with many existing marks — get a professional clearance
> search before spending on filings (see §2).

---

## TL;DR — the recommended route

1. **Register the _word_ “MercuryPitch”** in standard characters (not the logo first). A word mark
   protects the name in *any* font/color/logo — the broadest, most valuable protection.
2. **File your base mark at the EUIPO** (one filing = all 27 EU countries). You're EU-based, so this is
   your home registration and the anchor for everything else.
3. **Go global via the Madrid Protocol (WIPO)** — one international application, based on your EUTM,
   that designates the US, UK, and any other markets you want, administered through EUIPO as your
   “Office of Origin.”
4. **File the US carefully** — either as a Madrid designation or directly at the USPTO. The US is strict
   and **requires a US-licensed attorney for foreign applicants**, plus proof of real use.
5. **Claim priority:** file the international/foreign applications **within 6 months** of the EU filing
   to backdate them to your EU filing date (Paris Convention).

## 1. What to register, and in which classes

### Mark type
- **Word mark — “MercuryPitch” (standard characters).** ✅ Do this first. Protects the name itself.
- **Figurative mark — the logo.** Optional, add later once you've chosen the final mark (one of the
  explorations). Protects the specific visual. Costs a separate filing/class fees.

### Goods & services (Nice Classification)
MercuryPitch is a browser-based (hosted) app + training tool, so three classes fit:
| Class | Covers | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **9** | Downloadable / recorded software; downloadable apps | The app as software |
| **42** | SaaS; providing online non-downloadable software | It runs in the browser / is hosted |
| **41** | Education, training, entertainment; providing online training | Vocal pitch training & practice |

Filing 9 + 42 + 41 gives solid coverage for a music-training SaaS. You can trim to 9 + 41 to save cost,
but 42 is the most accurate for a web app — discuss with your attorney.

## 2. Clearance search (do this BEFORE filing)

“Mercury” is heavily used (Mercury Marine, Mercury Insurance, **Mercury Records** in music, etc.). The
full mark **“MercuryPitch”** in software/training classes is likely distinct, but **the music/entertainment
class (41) is where a conflict is most plausible** — so search carefully there.

Free databases to check first (knockout search):
- **EUIPO — eSearch plus / TMview** — https://www.tmdn.org/tmview (searches EU + many national offices)
- **WIPO — Global Brand Database** — https://branddb.wipo.int (international registrations)
- **USPTO — Trademark Search** — https://tmsearch.uspto.gov
- **UK IPO** — https://www.gov.uk/search-for-trademark
- Plain web + app-store + domain/social search for common-law uses.

Search: exact “MercuryPitch”, “Mercury Pitch”, and “Mercury” within classes 9/41/42. If anything close
turns up in your classes/markets, get a professional clearance opinion before filing.

## 3. Step-by-step

### Phase A — EU base registration (EUIPO)
1. **Clear** the mark (§2).
2. **File the EUTM** online at https://euipo.europa.eu — word mark “MercuryPitch”, classes 9/41/42,
   with a clear list of goods/services (use the EUIPO’s Harmonised Database picker to avoid objections).
3. **Examination** — EUIPO checks for absolute grounds (descriptiveness, etc.). “MercuryPitch” is a
   coined/suggestive term, so it should pass.
4. **Publication + opposition** — a **3-month** window for third parties to oppose.
5. **Registration** — if unopposed, typically **~4–6 months** total. Protection lasts **10 years,
   renewable** indefinitely.

### Phase B — Global expansion (Madrid Protocol, via WIPO)
6. Once you have the EUTM application/registration as your **base mark**, file an **International
   Application** through EUIPO (your Office of Origin) → forwarded to **WIPO**.
7. **Designate** the countries/regions you want: **US, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, South Korea,
   Switzerland, China**, etc. (~130 members). One application, one currency (CHF), one renewal.
8. Each designated office **examines under its own law** within 12–18 months and can refuse; you respond
   (often needing a **local attorney**, e.g., in the US).
9. **Dependency caveat:** for the first **5 years**, the international registration depends on the EU
   base — if the EUTM falls, the international one can too (“central attack”). After 5 years it becomes
   independent.

> **UK note:** the EUTM **no longer covers the UK** (post-Brexit). Designate the UK separately via Madrid,
> or file directly at the UK IPO.

### Phase C — United States (get this right)
The US is a big market and the strictest office:
- **Foreign applicants must appoint a US-licensed attorney** — mandatory.
- The US is **use-based**: you file on an **intent-to-use (1(b))** or **use-in-commerce (1(a))** basis and
  must eventually submit a **specimen** (proof the mark is used in US commerce). Madrid designations to the
  US (66(a)) still require a statement of intent to use.
- Expect possible **office actions**; US examiners scrutinize the goods/services wording.
- You can reach the US **via Madrid** (cheaper if filing many countries) **or file directly at USPTO**
  (often smoother for the US specifically). Many founders file EU + direct US, and Madrid for the rest.

---

## 4. Priority (don’t miss the 6-month window)
Under the **Paris Convention**, if you file your foreign/international applications **within 6 months** of
your first (EU) filing, they get the **EU filing date** as their priority date — blocking anyone who
filed in between. So: file EU first, then Madrid/US within 6 months.

## 6. Practical checklist
- [ ] Clearance search — especially class 41 (music/entertainment) for “Mercury” conflicts.
- [ ] Decide classes (9 / 41 / 42) and write tight goods/services descriptions.
- [ ] File **EUTM word mark** “MercuryPitch”.
- [ ] Within **6 months**: file **Madrid** (designate US, UK, +targets) and/or **direct US** with a US attorney.
- [ ] Later: file the **figurative (logo) mark** once the final logo is chosen.
- [ ] Use **™** now (unregistered); switch to **®** only after registration, per country.
- [ ] Lock domains/socials (you have mercurypitch.com + about.mercurypitch.com ✅).
- [ ] Keep authorship/vector source files for the logo (copyright evidence — see BRAND.md §7).
- [ ] Diary the **10-year renewals** and US **§8/§15 declarations of use** (years 5–6, and at renewal).

## 7. Who can do the filing for you
- **DIY:** EUIPO’s online “Fast Track” is genuinely usable for a word mark in the EU.
- **Online services:** e.g., trademark-filing platforms (bundle search + filing) — fine for straightforward
  EU/US filings, less so for contested marks.
- **A trademark attorney / IP firm:** best for the global Madrid strategy, the US, and if the clearance
  search surfaces any “Mercury” conflicts. Given you want *worldwide* coverage, budget for one.
