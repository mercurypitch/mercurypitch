The idea

Liquid Precision

Mercury is quicksilver — a liquid metal that is fluid, reflective, and responds instantly. That's exactly what the product does: it turns your voice into something you can see, in real time, with sub-cent accuracy. Mercury is also the fastest planet and the messenger god who, in myth, invented the lyre. Speed, communication, music, liquid metal — all in one word.

Copyright & production

Copyright-clean pipeline

The one rule that matters: for a logo you want a trademark, not a copyright — and you want clean vector. MidJourney is superb for imagery and ideation, but the wrong final step for a registrable logo.

Why: a purely AI-generated image generally can't be registered for copyright (no human authorship), so you may not be able to stop others copying a raw AI logo. A trademark protects your brand use regardless — and hand-drawn vector is both copyrightable and razor-sharp at favicon sizes.

Ideate in MidJourney

Explore droplet / liquid-M directions with the prompts above. Treat outputs as mood & reference.

Rebuild as original vector

Redraw the chosen mark in Illustrator / Figma / Inkscape — like the SVGs shipped in docs/branding/logo. Human-authored = copyrightable.

Trademark the name & mark

Knockout-search “MercuryPitch” (USPTO TESS / EUIPO) in software (cl. 9) & education (cl. 41), then file the wordmark + logo.

Use MJ for the surround

Hero art, backgrounds, OG & social — where “can’t copyright the raster” barely matters and speed wins.

Keep records & the right plan

Save prompts, seeds & vector sources. Companies > $1M/yr revenue need MidJourney Pro/Mega to use outputs commercially — verify current terms.
